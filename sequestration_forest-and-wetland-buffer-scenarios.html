Forest & Wetland Buffer Scenarios

What would Illinois look like if we added forest or wetland buffers along the rivers and streams in the state?

What the Scenarios Look Like

This map shows the potential results of restoring forests and grasslands to line the waterways. These buffers would protect water quality and fish habitat, while increasing carbon sequestration and providing other ecosystem services like flood protection and wildlife corridors.

Use the + and - buttons to zoom in or out to look at different parts of the state; use the buttons on the left to turn different layers on and off.

Estimated increase in carbon sequestration under landcover change scenarios

Adding ecological buffers along rivers and streams will increase the amount of carbon dioxide that is sequestered in the state and increase the acreage of nature conserved in the state.
