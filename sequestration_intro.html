Background & Context

Natural areas throughout Illinois provide places for recreation, while also protecting biodiversity and absorbing carbon dioxide from the atmosphere. By taking care of forests, wetlands, prairies and agricultural land, we can secure the well-being of generations to come.

To inform decisions about future plans, we have analyzed how much land is currently protected in Illinois and how much carbon is sequestered in natural areas and working lands in the state. We have compared the carbon sequestration of current landcover with a historic baseline and projections for future scenarios.
